Home Loan EMI Calculation Explained

When you take a home loan, you repay it in the form of EMIs (Equated Monthly Installments). Each EMI consists of two components: principal repayment and interest payment. Understanding how EMI is calculated helps you plan better and save lakhs in interest.

EMI Formula

EMI = P × R × (1+R)N / [(1+R)N − 1]

P = Loan Amount
R = Monthly Interest Rate (Annual Rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100)
N = Number of Monthly Installments

Example Calculation

Let’s assume:

  • Loan Amount: ₹1,00,00,000
  • Interest Rate: 7.5% per year
  • Tenure: 20 years (240 months)

The EMI will be approximately ₹80,559 per month. Over 20 years, the total interest paid can exceed ₹93 lakhs. That means you repay almost double the loan amount.

Why Interest Is So High

In the initial years of your loan, most of your EMI goes toward paying interest, not principal. That is why reducing principal early can create massive savings.

How To Finish Your Home Loan Early

1. Make Annual Prepayments

Even a yearly prepayment of ₹1,00,000 can reduce your tenure by 3–5 years and save several lakhs in interest.

2. Increase EMI Every Year

If your salary increases by 5–10% annually, increase your EMI by 5%. This drastically reduces total interest.

3. Use Bonuses To Reduce Principal

Instead of spending bonuses, use them to reduce loan principal.

4. Choose Shorter Tenure If Affordable

Lower tenure means higher EMI but much lower total interest paid.

Impact of ₹1,00,000 Annual Prepayment

For a ₹1 crore loan at 7.5%:

  • You can close your 20-year loan in around 14–16 years.
  • You can save ₹20–30 lakhs in total interest.

Smart Strategy

The best strategy is:

  • Keep EMI affordable
  • Make disciplined annual prepayments
  • Increase EMI whenever income increases

Becoming debt-free early gives financial freedom and peace of mind.
